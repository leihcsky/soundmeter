Hi there! I'm an independent developer who built Sound Meter. Thank you for visiting and using this tool.

I created this project because I wanted a simple, privacy-focused way to check noise levels without downloading sketchy apps or dealing with aggressive ads. Like many of you, I've become increasingly aware of how environmental noise affects our healthâ€”from sleep quality to long-term hearing preservation.

Why I Built This

A while back, I found myself in a surprisingly loud coffee shop, trying to focus. I wanted to know just how loud it was, but every website I found was either cluttered, required a sign-up, or wanted to install something.

I realized that with modern web technology, we don't need dedicated apps for this. Your browser is powerful enough to do the job. So, I built Sound Meter with a few core principles in mind:

  • Privacy First: What happens in your browser stays in your browser. No audio is ever recorded or sent to a server.
  • Simplicity: No logins, no complex setups. Just open the page and see the decibels.
  • Accessibility: It should work on your phone, tablet, or laptopâ€”anywhere with a microphone and a browser.

A Note on Accuracy

I want to be transparent with you: this is a reference tool, not a medical device.

I've done my best to calibrate the algorithms, but the accuracy ultimately depends on your device's hardware. A professional sound level meter costs hundreds of dollars for a reason. This tool is great for getting a general idea ("Is this rock concert too loud?" or "Is my workspace quiet enough?"), but please don't use it for safety compliance or medical diagnosis.

Tech Stack

For the curious developers out there, this site runs entirely on the client side using the Web Audio API. It analyzes the raw audio stream from your microphone in real-time to calculate the Root Mean Square (RMS) amplitude, which is then converted to decibels. It's lightweight, fast, and secure.
